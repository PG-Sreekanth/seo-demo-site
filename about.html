The Forgotten Grain

Three generations ago, millets were the staple food across Southern India. But the green revolution replaced these hardy, drought-resistant crops with water-intensive rice and wheat.

We started Milleth after witnessing the dual crises of rising diabetes rates in our cities and depleting groundwater in our villages. We realized that bringing back native grains solves both problems simultaneously.

By guaranteeing fair prices to farmers who pledge to use zero synthetic chemicals, we are rebuilding the ancient grain supply chain from the soil up.
